THE GLOBAL HUMAN DAY

How does the average human spend their day?

According to a paper published in 2023, this is precisely how.

Across the world, nearly 8 billion people divide their lives into the same fixed container: 24 hours a day. That limit makes time one of humanity’s most important constraints. Every choice about how people sleep, work, eat, move, care, socialize, or rest shapes what is possible at a global scale. How they are spent shapes not only individual lives, but also economies, societies, and environmental change across borders.

With 8 billion people in the world, humanity has 190 billion hours to spend every day.

The Administrative Tax On Life

Wealthier developed countries spend dramatically more time allocating daily life logistics, perhaps the main bureaucratic overhead of modern life.

Developing countries, such as India and Lebanon spend much less time managing life.

What Gets Displaced?

In wealthier countries, time spent on everyday logistics often share a negative relationship with social time.

Unsurprisingly, developing countries such as Eritrea and Morocco that saved on the allocation time often socialized more.

But categories can hide culture. In Western and Southern Europe, social life may not only appear as “socialising.”

Time spent eating may also carry social meaning, especially in cultures where eating together is part of daily life.

Hence, it's important to consider context when it comes to analyzing time use.

What does this mean for us?

The point is not that certain activities tasks are bad. The problem is that we often do not notice how much time they quietly take.

But at national scale, these small acts add up. If everyone in a country spent just one more minute a day socializing, that would become millions of extra hours of human connection every year.

A society is partly what it makes time for. This time-based view offers a different way to ask what humanity is doing, and what we might choose to do differently. How will you spend your time?
